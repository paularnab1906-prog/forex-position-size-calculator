What is Position Sizing?

Position sizing is the process of determining how much capital to allocate to a single trade. Proper position sizing helps manage risk by ensuring no single trade can significantly impact your account balance.

The key is to risk only a small percentage of your account per trade (typically 1-2%) to preserve capital during losing streaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a pip in forex?

A pip is the smallest price movement in forex trading, typically 0.0001 for most pairs. It measures the change in value between two currencies.

How much should I risk per trade?

Most traders risk 1-2% of their account balance per trade. This helps preserve capital during losing streaks and allows for long-term trading.

What is a good risk reward ratio?

A risk-reward ratio of 1:2 or better is generally considered good. This means your potential profit is at least twice your risk amount.

Can I use this calculator for crypto trading?

Yes, the calculator supports crypto pairs like BTC/USD with appropriate pip multipliers for accurate calculations.
